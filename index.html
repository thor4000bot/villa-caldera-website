Oia, Santorini

Where the Aegean Meets the Sky

Perched on the volcanic rim above Santorini's caldera, Villa Caldera is a four-bedroom private estate with an infinity pool that dissolves into 180° of open sea. The property is designed in the Cycladic tradition — whitewashed stone, vaulted ceilings, cool interiors that breathe in the island heat — and finished to a standard that matches the view.


Oia is a 12-minute walk. The airport is 25 minutes. Between the two, you have the caldera to yourselves: sunrise over the Aegean, blue-domed evenings, and a pool that catches the last light of every day.

The Pool

An Infinity Edge Over Open Water

The 12-metre heated infinity pool aligns perfectly with the caldera rim, vanishing into the Aegean at the horizon. There is no more dramatic swimming pool on the island — and it is entirely private.

Submerged sunbeds at the shallow end, a pool bar along the southern edge, and a sun terrace with daybeds facing the volcanic islands of Nea Kameni. Sunrise to sunset, the light changes every hour.

The Interiors

Cycladic Architecture, Refined

Four bedroom suites with vaulted ceilings and cave-cut walls keep the spaces naturally cool through the Aegean summer. Each suite opens onto a private terrace — two directly onto the caldera.

The main living space is a single open volume: kitchen, dining, and sitting room flowing into one another under a 4-metre arch, with floor-to-ceiling glass facing the sea.

The Location

On the Rim of the Caldera, Oia

Oia is the island's most celebrated village and the setting for Santorini's famous sunsets. Villa Caldera sits on the caldera-facing cliffside, a 12-minute walk from the village centre.
